# HoofStock (Cattle Investment SaaS)

A distinct Goji Labs product, currently in the discovery phase. HoofStock is a SaaS and marketplace platform for fractional cattle investment across the full lifecycle, from birth or purchase through feedlot finishing to auction, with a US based operating ranch partner and a licensed insurance partner. The full lifecycle program runs under the name Born to Plate.

## Status

Discovery. The current artifacts in this folder are the discovery outline (in Word and Markdown) and a generator script. They define the partnership scope, the three phase investment model, the phase based cost economics, and the platform modules. Financial projections, market sizing, and legal engagement are still to be completed in the first business plan draft.

## What it is

Investors enter at any of three phases, each a distinct tranche with its own upfront cost budget, risk profile, and exit gate. Risk declines as cattle mature, shifting from high early life mortality toward market price exposure at finish. Pool value tracks weight gain against the phase budget. The platform earns from phase processing fees, pool origination, marketplace activity, insurance placement, and recurring rancher subscriptions.

## Key open questions to resolve before the business plan

These are the items most likely to shape the structure, drawn from the discovery review.

1. Entity separation. Keep the SaaS and marketplace company separate from the entity that sponsors and operates the investment pools. The first is clean B2B infrastructure. The second is a securities sponsor. Conflating them undermines the infrastructure posture.
2. NAV methodology. Define how a living asset is marked at each gate, how often weight is measured, which price reference is used, and what independent valuation and haircut apply, since investors exit at gate NAV.
3. Secondary liquidity. Clarify whether phase gate exits are primary rolls and redemptions or true secondary trades, given Reg D resale restrictions.
4. Investor base. Decide between accredited only under 506(c) and a retail path under Reg A+ or Reg CF, since this sets the real market size.
5. Tokenization scope. Decide whether the certificate is a traceability record or the transferable security wrapper. If the latter, identity gating and a transfer agent are required.
6. Concentration. The single lease and single ranch partner are an enterprise level single point of failure. Lease assignability to the SPV is load bearing and currently unconfirmed.

## Cross product synergy

This product can draw on Corralign for accredited investor identity and holder whitelisting, and on the Agent Orchestration platform for operations. It shares the infrastructure versus fund thesis with the Goji Crypto platform. These are options to record, not current dependencies.

## Note

The discovery document states that it is not a securities offering document, which is accurate for that document. The product it describes does involve securities offerings through the pools, structured under Reg D. The distinction matters and is the reason for open question 1.
